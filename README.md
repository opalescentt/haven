# Project Description - Haven
Deployed website: [here]

Haven is a platform concept created to address the communication gaps that occur during foster care placement, particularly in the first 24 hours when caregivers must make quick, high-stakes decisions with limited information. Caregivers are often asked to consider placements with incomplete summaries, missing behavioral or trauma-related context, and delayed access to essential records such as education and healthcare information. These gaps exist largely because information transfer depends on individual caseworker capacity and fragmented systems across organizations.

Structural barriers further complicate the process. Foster caregivers are frequently excluded from care-relevant information because they are not legal guardians, and confidentiality regulations such as HIPAA and FERPA restrict access to useful context. As a result, caregivers spend significant time chasing information, feel unprepared at placement, and must sometimes decide whether to accept a placement within 24 hours without the knowledge needed to make an informed choice.

Haven’s MVP is intentionally scoped to reduce these gaps by centralizing access to referrals, resources, and community engagement opportunities in one secure platform. Rather than replacing existing child welfare systems, the MVP focuses on improving access, awareness, and connection—helping caregivers understand what information is available, where to go for support, and how to navigate placement more confidently.
